How to Make Kheer

Kheer Recipe

Kheer, also known as payasam or payesh, is a pudding or porridge popular in the Indian subcontinent, usually made by boiling milk, sugar or jaggery, and rice. It can be additionally flavoured with dried fruits, nuts, cardamom and saffron. Instead of rice, it may contain cracked wheat, vermicelli, sago or tapioca.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/2 cup rice (washed and soaked)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon raisins

Steps to Make Kheer

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, boil the milk on medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  2. Once the milk boils, add the soaked rice.
  3. Let the rice cook on low heat while stirring occasionally for about 40 minutes.
  4. Add sugar and cook for another 10-15 minutes until the kheer thickens.
  5. Add the chopped nuts, cardamom powder, saffron, and raisins. Stir well.
  6. Simmer for another 5 minutes, then remove from heat.
  7. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with more nuts!
