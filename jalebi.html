How to Make Jalebi

Jalebi Recipe

Jalebi is a traditional Indian sweet known for its crispy texture and tangy-sweet flavor. It is made from a fermented batter, deep-fried into circular shapes, and then soaked in sugar syrup.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water (for syrup)
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps to make Jalebi

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, rice flour, and baking powder. Add the yogurt and mix well. Gradually add water to form a smooth, slightly thick batter. Cover and let it ferment for about 8-12 hours or overnight.
  2. For the syrup, combine sugar and water in a pan. Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally until the sugar is completely dissolved. Add saffron and cardamom powder. Let the syrup simmer for a few minutes and then turn off the heat.
  3. Heat oil in a pan or deep fryer over medium heat. Pour the fermented batter into a piping bag or squeeze bottle with a small nozzle.
  4. Once the oil is hot, pipe the batter in circular or spiral shapes into the oil. Fry until golden and crisp on both sides. Remove the jalebis with a slotted spoon and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup.
  5. Let the jalebis soak in the syrup for a minute or two, then transfer them to a plate. Serve warm or at room temperature.
