How to Make Rasgulla

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom seeds (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon rose water (optional)

Steps to Make Rasgulla

  1. Boil the milk in a pan over medium heat. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  2. Once the milk comes to a boil, add the lemon juice or vinegar slowly while stirring gently. This will curdle the milk. Remove from heat.
  3. Strain the curdled milk through a muslin or cheesecloth to separate the curds from the whey. Rinse the curds under cold water to remove the acidic taste.
  4. Press the curds gently to remove excess water. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Knead the curds for 5-7 minutes to make a smooth dough. Divide the dough into small balls and set aside.
  6. In a separate pan, add sugar and water to make the syrup. Heat it until the sugar dissolves completely. Add cardamom seeds and rose water if using.
  7. Carefully drop the dough balls into the boiling syrup. Cook them on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes until they become soft and spongy. The rasgullas will expand and float on the surface when theyâ€™re done.
  8. Let the rasgullas cool in the syrup for a few hours before serving. Enjoy chilled or at room temperature!
