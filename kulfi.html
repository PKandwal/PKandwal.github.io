How to Make Kulfi

Kulfi Recipe

Kulfi is a traditional Indian frozen dessert that’s creamy, rich, and full of flavor. It’s often described as a denser and creamier version of ice cream.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (e.g., almonds, pistachios) (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • Kulfi molds or small cups for freezing

Steps to Make Kulfi

  1. In a large pan, heat the milk over medium heat. Stir frequently to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  2. Bring the milk to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until it reduces by about half. This should take around 15-20 minutes.
  3. Add the sugar and continue to cook, stirring, until it dissolves completely.
  4. Mix in the sweetened condensed milk, heavy cream, cardamom powder, saffron (if using), and rose water (if using). Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring frequently.
  5. If using nuts, add them to the mixture and stir well.
  6. Remove the pan from the heat and let the mixture cool slightly.
  7. Pour the mixture into kulfi molds or small cups. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap.
  8. Freeze the kulfi for at least 6 hours or overnight until fully set.
  9. To serve, remove the kulfi from the molds by running warm water over the outside of the mold or gently tapping the sides. Enjoy!
